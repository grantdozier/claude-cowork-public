Privacy Policy

Chase Group Construction LLC — Internal Business Tools
Effective date: May 21, 2026

1. Overview

This policy describes how Chase Group Construction LLC ("the Company") collects, uses, and protects data processed through its internal AI assistant platform ("the Tool"). The Tool is not a public-facing product and is not available to the general public.

2. Data Collected

The Tool accesses the following categories of data, all of which are already held by the Company:

  • Microsoft 365 data — employee email, calendar events, SharePoint files, and OneDrive documents, accessed through delegated OAuth under each user's own credentials.
  • QuickBooks Online data — vendor bills, payments, and job cost information, accessed through the Company's own QuickBooks account.
  • Internal audit corpus — structured data derived from the above sources and stored in the Company's Azure SQL database.

3. How Data Is Used

  • To answer employee queries about project status, finances, documents, and schedules.
  • To generate operational reports and briefings for internal use.
  • To maintain a historical record of project documents and communications for the Company's own audit and compliance purposes.

4. Data Storage

All data is stored within the Company's Microsoft Azure subscription (region: US). No data is transmitted to or stored by third-party AI providers beyond what is necessary to process a specific query (e.g., a document excerpt sent to an AI model for classification). Anthropic's data usage policies apply to those API calls.

5. Access Controls

Access to Tool data is governed by per-user access scopes defined in the Company's internal configuration. Employees can only query data within their authorized scope. Executive-confidential and HR data are restricted to designated roles.

6. Data Sharing

The Company does not sell, rent, or share Tool data with third parties, except as required by law or as necessary to operate the underlying cloud services (Microsoft Azure, Anthropic API, Intuit QuickBooks API).

7. Retention

Data in the audit corpus is retained in accordance with the Company's general records-retention policy. Users may request deletion of their personal data by contacting the administrator.
